About the Fellowship

Data Science in Service of Equity

As a 2025–2026 CAnD3 Fellow, I receive advanced training in population data science, quantitative methods, and policy-relevant research — bridging my background in nursing and sociology with data analytics to produce rigorous, evidence-based insights on health equity and population outcomes.

The fellowship builds on my doctoral work examining the health outcomes of children and youth exposed to adversity in Canada, equipping me to translate population research into policy-actionable insights.

Training Focus
Interdisciplinary data science, policy analysis, and knowledge mobilization applied to aging societies and population health. Methods include multivariate regression, longitudinal analysis, national survey data, and data visualization.

CAnD3 DataViz Shark Tank

Public Perceptions of Racial Inequality & Educational Opportunity

In collaboration with Jiawen Li · GSS · WTSSPS-weighted · % "Yes" by Census division

2014 — Baseline

Study Context

This data visualization project was completed in collaboration with Jiawen Li as part of the CAnD3 DataViz Shark Tank Challenge. Using data from the American General Social Survey (GSS), we examined whether Americans attribute racial economic inequality to limited educational opportunities — a key mechanism through which socioeconomic advantage and disadvantage are transmitted across generations. In 2014, agreement was concentrated in the Northeast, with lower levels across the South and West.

2022 — Key Findings

What Changed

By 2022, agreement had shifted markedly westward. Black respondents showed the largest increase in agreement (+10.4 percentage points between 2016 and 2022), while White respondents remained stable at around 50% and respondents categorized as Other saw a notable decline (−13.6 percentage points). These diverging trends suggest that perceptions of education as a driver of racial inequality are shaped by region, lived experience, and the broader historical and political moment.

Policy Work

Policy Brief: Caregiver Sleep & Health Systems

This policy brief examines how chronic sleep deprivation among informal caregivers contributes to burnout, long-term health risks, and health system strain — and what targeted policy interventions can help.

Prepared as part of CAnD3 training in knowledge mobilization and policy translation, the brief draws on national survey data and international comparators to make evidence-based recommendations for Canadian governments.

Download PDF
Authors
Rosario Aronica, Fabio Robibaro, Ruth Lue, Leah Houseman
Policy Question
How does chronic sleep deprivation among carers of medically complex and ageing individuals contribute to caregiver burnout and long-term health risks?
Recommendation
Coordinated strategy combining targeted respite expansion with system-level supports for high-risk caregivers

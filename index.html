About

Bridging nursing, sociology & population health

Welcome to my e-portfolio. I am a PhD student in Sociology and an Assistant Teaching Professor in Nursing at the University of New Brunswick, with a strong commitment to health equity and nursing education.

This space reflects my journey, values, and ongoing work in teaching, research, and global health leadership — integrating equity-oriented, culturally safe, and globally informed approaches to health and education.

Get in Touch
Teaching Philosophy

Equity-oriented, culturally safe, and evidence-informed nursing education that prepares future nurses to serve diverse communities.

Research Approach

Population-level, data-driven inquiry at the intersection of sociology, nursing, and public health — foregrounding lived experiences and systemic inequities.

Global Commitment

As Global Health Lead, integrating international perspectives and Indigenous health frameworks into curriculum and scholarly work.

Research Focus

Areas of Scholarly Interest

⚖️

Health Equity

Examining structural and systemic barriers to health, with emphasis on marginalized populations and the conditions that produce health disparities across communities.

🌍

Social Determinants of Health

Investigating how social, economic, and environmental factors — housing, income, race, gender — shape health outcomes at the individual and population level.

📊

Population Data Science

Applying quantitative and mixed methods approaches through the CAnD3 Fellowship to produce policy-relevant evidence on population health and well-being.

🌿

Indigenous Health

Engaging with Indigenous health frameworks, decolonial methodologies, and culturally safe approaches in both research and nursing education curriculum.

🏥

Nursing Education

Developing equity-oriented, globally informed curricula that prepare nursing students to practice in diverse clinical and community settings.

🌐

Global Health

Leading global health initiatives and integrating international health perspectives and cross-cultural competencies into undergraduate nursing education.

Fellowship

CAnD3 Fellowship

The Consortium on Analytics for Data-Driven Decision-Making (CAnD3) provides advanced training in population data science, quantitative research methods, and policy-relevant scholarship.

As a CAnD3 Fellow, I receive specialized training that bridges my background in nursing and sociology with cutting-edge data analytics — enabling me to produce rigorous, evidence-based insights on health equity and population outcomes.
